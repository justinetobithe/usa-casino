Factors to consider when choosing a slot game include the number of reels you want, the number of paylines (the more the better!), and the volatility of a particular slot game. Volatility refers to the frequency and size of each game’s payout. A game with low volatility pays out a lower amount, but in greater frequency.

A slot with high volatility pays out much larger sums, but very rarely. If you want to start out slowly at first and not risk too much money, play slots with a low to medium volatility. For high rollers who have a larger bankroll and want a higher risk (and bigger payout), choose slots with a high volatility rating. You can find the details of a slot game’s volatility in the game descriptions.
