Online Baccarat Guide

Baccarat is surely one of the greatest real money games available on the casino floor today thanks to its very low house edge, around 1%. It requires little to no skill to play and is easy to learn. The idea of the game is to get a total value of nine (or as close to it as possible) without going over, based on two cards drawn to both player and dealer (or banker).

Aces count for one, 2-9 are taken at face value, and tens/face cards have a value of zero. Players can choose to bet on the player, dealer, or a tie. It is impossible to go over or “bust” like in blackjack, and the dealer is required to hit based on a predetermined set of rules.

Avoid Patterns

Many real money baccarat players rely on patterns and supposed hot and cold streaks within the game. Baccarat tables will record the results of a game (number of wins for player/banker). However, in baccarat, the odds are similar to determining the outcome of a coin toss. Each result is a separate event.

Bet on Banker

Despite a 5% commission, this is the best bet you can make in baccarat due to a very low house edge of 1.06%. The banker bet will win 50.68% of the time and thus bring consistent returns. This fraction of a percentage will translate into noticeable steady gains over time.

Forget the Tie Bet

This is the worst bet that a player can make in baccarat. Although an 8 to 1 payout might seem rewarding, the house edge on the tie bet is 14.4%! If you manage to pull off a tie bet, the payout is lucrative, which is why it is so appealing. But the risk of losing is exceptionally high.

Keep it Brief

Real money baccarat is all about short winning streaks followed by cutting your losses. Unlike a game like blackjack, which can grant patient players long and lucrative upswings, baccarat is more of a sprint than a marathon. Ride a wave of fortune for as long as possible then cash in your chips.

Mind the Tables

A 5% commission is paid to the dealer for all wins on banker bets. However, do not assume that all casinos follow this rule. Some casinos have tables that collect 10% or even 25% commission on banker bet wins. Mind the table rules listed for the casino of your choice.

Manage your Bankroll

This should come as a no-brainer, but one overlooked aspect of online gambling, or gambling in general, is successfully managing ones’ bankroll. The key is to set a loss limit, and not to exceed it once you have laid out a proper strategy. Don’t get greedy or chase your losses.

Types Of Bets In Baccarat

Now that you have a better feel for how the game of baccarat works, let’s take a quick look at betting and some real money baccarat strategy that you can incorporate into your game. Baccarat uses three types of bets:

Banker Bet

This is the safest bet that you can make in baccarat. The house edge on the banker bet is 1.06%. The standard rule is that the player must pay a 5% commission to the banker on each winning bet, and even with the commission, your odds of winning are better than any other bet.

Player Bet

The house edge on the player bet is 1.24%. The odds are a little higher, but the fact that the banker bet utilizes the aforementioned commission equalizes the advantage to the house.

Tie Bet

Though the tie bet offers a massive payout, any expert will tell you that the tie bet is not a bet worth making due to the staggeringly high house advantage of roughly 14.4%. The payout is 8:1, which is why the chances of winning are so low. However, some casinos utilize a 9:1 payout, which reduces the house edge to about 5%.

Baccarat Facts And Trivia

Origins of the game

The exact origins of baccarat are unclear. Variations of the game have existed throughout the world since at least the 19th century. Various sources claim the game was first introduced as early as the end of the 15th century by soldiers during the Franco-Italian War and the reign of Charles VII. A precursor known as Macao came from Asia during the 17th century. Today three popular versions of the game exist.

Mini-baccarat

Mini-baccarat can be found in many North American casinos today. It allows gamblers to play for lower stakes with the key difference being that only the dealer can touch the cards. When playing baccarat for real was introduced to The Sands Casino in Vegas, the casino lost over $250,000 in one night. Baccarat is the most popular game among high rollers, and high stakes rooms exist where table limits can hit over $100,000 a hand.

Three versions

Punto Banco, Chemin de Fer, and Baccarat Banque are the three versions of baccarat that exist in casinos today. The version immortalized in the famous 1962 James Bond film “Dr. No”, starring Sean Connery, is Chemin de Fer. This version is most popular in France and is a quicker version of the original game. The railway in French, “chemin de fer” was the quickest form of transport at the time of its conception.
